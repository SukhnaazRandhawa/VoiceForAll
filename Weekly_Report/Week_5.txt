Pre Meeting Report => 

Progress Since Last Meeting:

I have completed a literature review of recent sign language recognition systems and identified three implementation 
approaches: (1) Isolated Recognition - one sign per recording (89-97% accuracy but poor UX), 
(2) Pause-Based Continuous - multiple signs with pauses in one recording, with automatic segmentation, 
and (3) True Continuous - natural signing without pauses (state-of-the-art research problem, 
60-70% accuracy, high computational cost).

I think I should go for Option 2 (Pause-Based Continuous Recognition) as it balances usability with technical feasibility. 
Users click START, perform multiple signs with brief pauses, then click STOP. 
The system automatically detects pauses via hand velocity thresholds and segments the video for recognition. 
This approach achieves a natural user experience (one recording session) while maintaining high accuracy 
(target: 75-85%), builds upon proven methods with a novel segmentation contribution, and aligns with commercial 
systems like Google SignAll.

Technical Implementation for Option 2: 
Feature Extraction: MediaPipe Holistic (75 keypoints → 225D feature vectors per frame)
Recognition Model: Hybrid LSTM-GRU network (89.5% baseline accuracy)
Pause Detection: Frame-to-frame hand velocity algorithm with empirical threshold
Stack: Python 3.8+, TensorFlow/Keras, OpenCV, NumPy
Dataset: WLASL (50-word vocabulary for proof-of-concept)

Set up the environment and verify the MediaPipe feature extraction
