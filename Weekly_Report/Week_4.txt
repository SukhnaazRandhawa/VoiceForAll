What I achieved since our last meeting : 

Last week, I completed reading several research papers that covered static (isolated) sign language recognition. 
These papers focused on recognizing individual signs in isolation, where each sign is performed separately with 
clear start and end boundaries. The approaches primarily used MediaPipe for hand and pose keypoint extraction, 
combined with LSTM/GRU architectures for classification. This gave me a solid foundation in understanding the fundamental 
techniques for sign recognition.

I am now moving forward with reading new research papers that cover continuous sign language recognition. 
This is more challenging as it involves recognizing signs from continuous signing sequences without clear boundaries 
between signs—similar to how we speak in natural sentences. I'm particularly focusing on papers that discuss CTC-based 
approaches, sequence-to-sequence models, and transformer architectures for handling continuous signing.

Points of discussion for the meeting : 

My goal is to create a system that continuously records the user's signing and predicts complete sentences using 
NLP techniques, similar to natural conversation flow. So far, the research papers I have read primarily focus 
on static/isolated sign detection with relatively small vocabularies of around 100 signs or less. 
However, moving to continuous sign language recognition represents a significant jump in complexity, 
as it requires handling temporal sequences, sign boundaries, and larger vocabularies. 
Therefore, I am currently reading multiple research papers specifically focused on continuous sign language 
recognition to better understand the approaches, datasets, and architectures needed for this more challenging task.
